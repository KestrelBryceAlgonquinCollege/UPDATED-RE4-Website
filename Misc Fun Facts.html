Misc Fun Facts


This page is dedicated to random bits of information I found while I was researching for this website that didn't fit in any of the other categories, as most of these aren't exclusive to one singular release.

Outdated currency


The PTAS that you use to buy things from the Merchant are a real currency in Spain, but they were dropped in favor of the Euro in 2002, two years before this game takes place. Whether keeping the old currency was a conscious decision by Capcom to illustrate how isolated the inhabitants of the village are is unknown, but I personally think that the Merchant melts down all of coins you give him to sell the gold at an inflated price elsewhere.


Blatant misgendering (by accident???)


As this game takes place in Spain and the ganados are spanish-speaking, all of their voicelines are in spanish, and they stay the same no matter which character you're playing as. This wouldn't seem like it would be a problem, but the enemy voicelines were designed for Leon, who is male. Since spanish is a gendered language, all of the voicelines are conjugated as if they are speaking to a male, which doesn't make sense when you're playing as Ashley or Ada. There are quite a few voice lines that fall under this gendered umbrella, but the most common ones are: "Mátalo!" (kill him) and "Cojelo, cojelo, cojelo..." (Get him, get him, get him...). The correct conjugation for these phrases would be: "Mátala!" (kill her) and "Cogela, cogela, cogela..." (Get her, get her, get her...).
